04
How to measure

Three ways to measure the fence.
Use whichever is easiest — or a mix.

All three options produce the same result. The customer or field person just picks what works for their situation. On a computer only the map is available. On a phone all three work.

Computer + Phone
Draw on the satellite map

Click around the fence line on a satellite image of the property. The system measures the exact length automatically. On a computer this uses the Lantmäteriet API for precise property boundaries.

  • Most accurate — the map measures for you
  • Corners and sections marked as you draw
  • Lantmäteriet API for exact boundaries (computer)
  • Works in any browser — nothing to install
Phone only
Walk the fence with your phone

Open the app and physically walk along the fence line. The GPS on the phone traces every metre in real time. When done, the exact length is calculated automatically.

  • No guessing — GPS measures as you walk
  • Tap to mark corners, gates, and obstacles
  • Review the traced line and correct if needed
  • Eliminates the "100m that turned out to be 200m" problem
Phone only
Photo + pin measurement

Take a photo of each section, then tap a start point and drag to the end to mark the distance. The app measures using that line. Photos stay attached to the job record.

  • Tap a point, drag to the next — app measures the line
  • Photo taken per section as evidence
  • Works for customers who cannot draw or walk the line
  • Same structured output — no difference downstream
On a computer, only the map drawing is available. On a phone all three options work — and can be combined. For example: draw the main fence on the map, then walk a tricky section with GPS.

05
Getting the quote right

After measuring, the customer answers
a few short questions.

Once the measurements are in, the tool asks a short set of questions. The answers make sure the right materials end up on the truck — not the wrong ones.

The most important question is about terrain. The terrain type does not change the price — it changes what material gets loaded on the truck. Getting this wrong means a rebook and a lost day.

Example terrain question — customer picks one

Flat grass, easy to digConcrete footings, standard driver
Some slope, light vegetationLonger posts, brush clearing crew
Rocky or uneven groundRock anchors, no concrete
Significant slope, heavy vegetationMountain anchors, chainsaw crew
Extreme terrain or bedrockSpecialist equipment, extended brief

Other questions in the quote

How many gates?Count + width + type
Fence heightStandard or custom
Colour preferenceApplied as modifier
Corners and end postsAuto-counted from the map or entered manually
Terrain answerSets materials — not the price

All prices come live from Business Central every time. A price change in BC shows up in both tools immediately.

Crew dispatch

Quote approved. Job packet sent.

Today the job lives across WhatsApp, Feedly, and SMS. One missed message and the wrong crew shows up with the wrong material. This platform replaces all of that with two automatic channels.

Feedly — for your crew

Work order pushed to Feedly the moment a quote is approved.

No one writes anything manually. The work order is created automatically from the quote — with the date, site address, fence measurements, terrain type, and material list already filled in.

  • PDF has the date, address, fence plan, terrain notes, and materials

  • Share via WhatsApp in one tap
  • Subcontractors need nothing except a phone
Everything in one place. The quote, the order, the materials, and the crew brief all come from the same source. No syncing between apps, no version confusion, no missed messages.
